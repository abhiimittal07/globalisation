Project Title: Globalisation

What is Globalisation?

Definition: Globalisation is the process by which businesses, cultures, and economies become interconnected and operate on a global scale.

Key Features:

  • Increased international trade
  • Easier movement of people and goods
  • Rapid spread of technology and ideas
  • Global financial markets

Table: Globalisation – Then vs Now

Past: Letters, telegrams, ship travel, local trade

Now: Internet, flights, global trade, smartphones

Drivers of Globalisation

Main Drivers:

  1. Technology - Internet, smartphones, automation
  2. Transportation - Faster and cheaper shipping and air travel
  3. Trade Agreements - WTO, NAFTA, EU
  4. Multinational Companies - Apple, McDonald’s, Amazon

Example: Amazon sources products globally and delivers them in one day.

Positive Effects of Globalisation

Benefits:

  • Access to more goods and services
  • Cultural exchange and learning
  • More job opportunities in developing countries
  • Growth in technology and innovation

Examples: India (IT), China (Manufacturing), USA (Consumer goods), South Korea (Entertainment)

Negative Effects of Globalisation

Challenges:

  • Loss of local culture
  • Exploitation of cheap labor
  • Environmental damage due to mass production
  • Rich-poor gap may widen

Example: Fast fashion and poor factory conditions

Pros vs Cons: Jobs vs. Worker exploitation, Global goods vs. Local business loss
